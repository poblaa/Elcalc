User Manual - Fuel Consumption Calculator

1. Getting Started

This calculator helps you plan voyage fuel consumption based on route sections, engine RPM, environmental conditions, and auxiliary equipment usage.

2. Section Management

Activating a Section: Double-click on any section header to activate it. Only one section can be active at a time. The active section has a blue border.

Adding Sections: Click "Add Segment" button to create new route sections.

Removing Sections: Click the "Remove" button within each section (visible when you have 2+ sections).

Collapsing Sections: Click the arrow icon (▼) to collapse/expand section details.

3. Distance Input

Manual Entry: Type distance directly in the "Distance (nm)" field.

Map-Based Distance:

  • Add Pin: Click on the map to place waypoints
  • Move Pin: Click and hold, then drag to reposition
  • Remove Pin: Double-click on any pin to delete it

Note: Pins are only active for the currently selected section. Distance is automatically calculated using geographic coordinates (Haversine formula).

4. Main Engine Parameters

RPM: Engine revolutions per minute (45-123 RPM range)

Environmental Factor (0.75-1.25):

  • Good conditions (0.75-0.90): Calm seas, favorable weather
  • Model conditions (1.00): Standard operational conditions
  • Bad conditions (1.10-1.25): Rough seas, adverse weather

ME AVG: Displays the calculated main engine consumption rate corrected by environmental factor.

5. Environmental Factor Validation

⚠️ Important: When using environmental factors other than 1.00, verify your results against historical data:

  • Click "Data Chart" to view historical consumption data
  • Check if your red working point falls within the cloud density of blue historical points
  • If the red dot is far from the historical data cloud, your environmental factor may be incorrect

Note: The environmental factor system is under continuous improvement. Always cross-reference with historical data for accuracy.

6. Auxiliary Equipment

DG Consumption (0.13-0.18 mt/h):

  • Diesel Generator approximate consumption rate
  • Default: 0.155 mt/h for normal conditions
  • Increase slightly in summer when air conditioning load is higher
  • ⚠️ Only ONE DG is running at a time

Boiler Running (0-100%):

  • Percentage of section time that the boiler operates
  • Boiler nominal consumption: 0.060 mt/h (uses DO fuel)
  • Example: 50% means boiler runs half the section duration
  • Boiler consumption remains constant throughout the year

7. Time and Speed Calculation

You can input either time OR speed - the other will be calculated automatically:

  • If you enter Time: Speed = Distance ÷ Time
  • If you enter Speed: Time = Distance ÷ Speed

8. Results Interpretation

Consumption: Total HFO consumed in this section (Main Engine + DG)

HFO ROB: Heavy Fuel Oil Remaining On Board after this section

DO ROB: Diesel Oil Remaining On Board after this section (used by boiler)

⚠️ If HFO ROB > HFO Start, you'll see a red warning - check your starting fuel value.

9. About the Model

Data Collection Period: 28 January 2020 to 28 January 2026 (6 years)

Data Quality: Over 2,000 high-accuracy fuel readings selected using strict criteria:

  • Only events with duration longer than 5 hours
  • Main engine running time: 5 hours ± 20 minutes
  • Additional filtering conditions to ensure measurement accuracy

Model Equation:
y = 0.000124176621498486×(RPM²) - 0.00391529744030522×(RPM) + 0.104802913006673
where y = consumption [mt/h], RPM = engine revolutions per minute

Corrected Consumption: yc = y × Environmental Factor

10. Data Visualization

Click "Data Chart" to toggle between map and charts:

  • Model Consumption Chart: Blue curve showing the baseline model (45-124 RPM)
  • Historical Consumption Chart: Blue scatter points from actual measurements
  • Red Working Points: Your current section parameters plotted on charts

11. Tips & Best Practices

  • Always activate the section before placing pins on the map
  • Verify environmental factors against historical data cloud
  • Remember: Only ONE DG runs at a time - adjust consumption accordingly
  • Use boiler percentage for partial operation (e.g., 30% if running 3 hours out of 10)
  • Check HFO ROB warnings to ensure sufficient starting fuel
  • Summer operations: slightly increase DG consumption for AC load
